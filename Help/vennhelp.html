Diagram actions
 The Venn Diagram is altered by selecting either `Shading' or `Particular' from the radio button set and then clicking on an appropriate region of the image.
With `Shading' selected, clicking on an unshaded area will shade it, unless you have already reached the maximum number of shaded areas (four). Clicking on a shaded area will remove the shading.

With `Particular' selected, clicking in an unoccupied region or on the border between two unshaded regions will result in an `X' being positioned appropriately. Clicking on a region that already contains an X will remove that X. Otherwise clicking on a diagram which already contains an X will result in the X being moved unless the rules for constructing diagrams of syllogisms allow a second X to be placed in that position.

Other actions
 At any point the `Check' button may be selected; clicking on the diagram at this point will check the diagram against the premises of the syllogism. If the premises are accurately represented by the diagram you will be asked whether the syllogism is valid or invalid. Otherwise you will be presented with the opportunity to continue to modify the diagram.

The `Clear' button will return the diagram to the basic, unshaded 3-circle figure.
