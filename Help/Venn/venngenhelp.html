General information about Venn Diagrams

Venn Diagrams are used to represent the premises of a syllogism.

Apples Bland Things

Crunchy Things

Premises that describe general relationships between classes, such as that `All crunchy things are apples', are represented by shading out parts of the diagram that cannot be occupied if the premise is true.

Apples Bland Things

Premises that describe the existence of a particular object, such as `some Apples are bland', are represented by placing an X in the appropriate part of the diagram. Sometimes, the X must be placed on the border of two areas, representing uncertainty about its location.


Crunchy Things

The validity of a syllogism is checked by diagramming the premises and then looking to see whether the conclusion is represented by the constructed diagram.
