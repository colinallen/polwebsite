Step One: Bracketing Premises

The first step in diagraming arguments is to bracket each statement in the argument. In the box provided, simply position the cursor and type a "[" at the beginning of each statement and "]" at the end of each statement. You may check your work at any time by pressing the "Check Brackets" button.

Be careful not to modify the text of the argument itself as this may cause the program to reject your efforts, although adding extra punctuation, spaces, and extra line breaks will not typically have any effect. The "Remove Brackets" button can be used to restore the argument to its original, unbracketed state.

Step Two: Constructing the Diagram

The second step is to construct a representation of the relationships between statements in the argument. You will be initially presented with a table that has a single cell with the number 0, as shown at right. The first thing you probably want to do is change this zero to the number of the final conclusion of the argument that you are diagramming. Then you are ready to start expanding the diagram to include other statements in the argument.
Initial state of argument diagram table

Use the control buttons to generate and align the elements of the diagram. You may check your work at any point by pressing the "Check Diagram" button, which will provide feedback about elements that are incorrect or missing.

The web browser uses shaded backgrounds to play the role of the arrows in the textbook, i.e. to show the support relationships between statements. Statements should be vertically aligned with single supporters or placed anywhere beneath groups of supporters. Follow the darker shading to see which statements support those on the lower row.

Use small (tab) buttons on the left and right of the table to expand rows to left and right and to properly align statements and their supports (see example at right). A dot will appear in newly created cells to represent a tab space. Use mouse to click into table cells where you should replace a dot with a numbers (to represent a statement in the argument ), or enter v between independent supports, and + between interdependent) supports.

Remove a cell by clicking in with mouse and deleting all its contents (including invisible elements, if any).

Use Redraw/Recolor button to see the effects of changes. After a redraw the color or shading indicates the support relationships among statements.
